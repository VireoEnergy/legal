Solar Savings FAQ

Answers to the questions every solar household is asking

This is the most common frustration for solar owners, and it almost always comes down to one thing: timing. Your panels produce energy during the middle of the day, but most households use the bulk of their electricity in the morning and evening. The surplus solar gets exported to the grid for a low feed-in tariff, and then you buy that same energy back at full retail price a few hours later. The fix isn't more panels. It's using the energy you already generate at the right time. OnSun forecasts your daily solar production so you can shift washing, cooking, heating, and charging into your peak solar window and stop giving your energy away.
The most impactful thing you can do is increase your self-consumption rate: the percentage of solar energy you use in your home rather than exporting to the grid. Most households sit at 25-35% self-consumption without trying. By shifting flexible loads like washing machines, dryers, dishwashers, pool pumps, and EV chargers into the solar window, you can push this to 60-80%. That's the difference between saving a few dollars a week and saving several hundred dollars a year. OnSun makes this easy by giving you a 7-day production forecast and recommending the best times to schedule each appliance based on your specific system and local weather.
The textbook answer is 10am to 2pm, and on a clear summer day that's broadly right. But the real answer changes every day. Cloud cover, season, panel orientation, and even shading from nearby trees all affect when your panels produce the most energy. A partly cloudy day might have a narrow 2-hour window of strong production, while a clear winter day might peak earlier or later than summer. OnSun uses detailed weather and solar irradiance data for your exact location to predict your production curve each day. Instead of guessing, you get a specific recommendation like "Run your washing machine at 11am Wednesday" to help you hit the solar window.
Solar forecasting combines several data sources to predict how much electricity your panels will produce. It starts with weather forecasts (cloud cover, temperature, and humidity), then factors in your panel specifications (size, tilt, orientation) and your geographic location to calculate expected solar irradiance. OnSun uses state-of-the-art forecasting models that predict output at 15-minute to 1-hour intervals depending on your location. The result is a personalised production curve for each day of the coming week, so you can plan your energy usage around the sun rather than guessing.
