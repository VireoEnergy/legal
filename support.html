About OnSun

What OnSun Does

OnSun helps households forecast solar generation and run appliances at peak solar times to reduce grid imports. It provides weather-based forecasts, projected savings estimates, and scheduling suggestions to help you make the most of your solar system.

Note: OnSun does not control appliances automatically. You remain in control of when and how you operate your equipment.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

How do forecasts work?

OnSun uses weather data and solar forecasting algorithms to estimate your solar generation throughout the day. Forecasts consider factors like cloud cover, time of year, and your location. Because solar generation depends on many uncontrollable factors (weather, shading, and system performance), forecasts are estimates and real-world results may differ.

What forecast interval does OnSun use?

In most regions, forecasts are provided in hourly intervals. In areas with higher-resolution meteorological coverage, the models produce 15-minute interval forecasts. Your location determines the time resolution you see in the app. OnSun automatically uses the highest resolution available for your region.

How are savings calculated?

Savings estimates are based on your forecasted solar generation and typical energy rates. The app compares running appliances during peak solar times versus importing from the grid. These are projections only; actual savings will depend on your specific energy plan, usage patterns, and real-world solar output.

Subscription and billing

OnSun offers free and paid subscription tiers. Subscriptions are managed through your device's app store (Apple App Store or Google Play). This means:

  • Subscriptions auto-renew unless cancelled at least 24 hours before the end of the current period
  • Billing is handled by Apple or Google, so we do not process or store payment information
  • You can manage or cancel your subscription in your Apple ID or Google account settings
  • No refunds for partial periods, except where required by law

How to restore purchases

Open OnSun, go to Settings, and tap "Restore Purchases." Ensure you're signed in with the same Apple ID (iOS) or Google account (Android) used for the original purchase.

How does OnSun estimate appliance consumption?

OnSun estimates how much power each appliance uses with approximated load profiles. For many appliances (washing machines, dishwashers, clothes dryers, air conditioners, and pool pumps) the app builds a time-varying profile that approximates a typical cycle (for example a heater spike, a wash phase, rinse and spin stages, or compressor cycling) rather than treating consumption as a single flat block. Other appliances, such as ovens, heaters, or custom appliances, use a simpler steady ("block") profile.

These profiles are calibrated against typical, publicly available appliance data grouped by efficiency rating, but they are estimates, not measurements. Your real appliance can draw meaningfully more or less power depending on its model, age, settings, load size, water temperature, and operating conditions. For the most accurate picture, compare against your own utility data or smart meter where you have one. We don't directly meter or control your appliances.

How does OnSun estimate EV charging?

For EV charging, OnSun estimates a charging profile from your charger rating, battery capacity, and the current and target state of charge you enter. It applies a tapering curve (charging slows as the battery fills) and a typical charging-loss factor. This is an approximation only: real charging energy and time vary by vehicle, charger, battery temperature and age, and onboard charging limits, so your actual charge may differ from the estimate.

Can I get detailed profile scheduling?

If you need finer-grained scheduling (for example, to avoid peak tariff periods or coordinate with home battery cycling), please get in touch. This is supported by our architecture and community interest helps us prioritise it.

Configuration Guide

Configuring Your Solar Settings

For the most accurate forecasts, take a few minutes to configure your system settings. Here's what each setting means and how to find the right values.

System Size

The total capacity of your solar panels in kilowatts (kW). Shown on your solar quote, inverter display, or monitoring app.

Typical range: 3.0 to 10.0 kW

Common sizes: 6.6 kW (very popular), 5.0 kW, 10 kW for larger homes.

Panel Tilt

The angle your panels are tilted from horizontal. Flat roof = 0°, vertical wall = 90°.

Quick guide:

  • Typical pitched roof: 22° - 30°
  • Flat roof with tilt frames: 10° - 15°
  • Flat roof (no tilt): 0°

Your installer's documentation or your phone's level app can give you this.

Panel Direction (Azimuth)

The compass direction your panels face. 0° = North, 180° = South.

Common directions:

  • North: 0°, best for year-round generation
  • East: 90°, good morning sun
  • West: 270°, good afternoon sun
  • North-East: 45°
  • North-West: 315°

Use your phone's compass while standing in front of your panels.

Performance Ratio

A factor accounting for real-world losses: shading, dust, inverter efficiency, temperature, and wiring losses. Think of it as overall system efficiency.

Typical values:

  • Excellent system: 0.85 - 0.90
  • Good system (default): 0.80 - 0.85
  • System with shading: 0.75 - 0.80

Most users can leave this at 0.85. Lower it if you have significant shading or an older system.

Base Load

Your home's minimum power consumption (in kW) when no major appliances are running, such as fridges, standby devices, routers, etc.

How to estimate:

  • Check your smart meter or inverter app late at night when everything is off
  • Typical homes: 0.3 - 0.6 kW (300-600W)
  • Homes with always-on AC or pool pumps: 0.8 - 1.5 kW

This helps OnSun calculate how much solar is available for your appliances.

Inverter Limit (Optional)

The maximum AC output your inverter can deliver. Only needed if your inverter is significantly smaller than your panel capacity.

Example: 8 kW of panels with a 5 kW inverter would have a limit of 5.0 kW.

If your inverter and panel sizes are similar, leave this blank.

Location

Your home's location determines sun angles and local weather data. Accurate location means accurate forecasts.

Tips:

  • Use the location button to auto-detect
  • If you're on the edge of a climate zone, precision matters more
  • Update this if you move or use the app at a holiday home

Prefer not to share your location?

You can manually enter your latitude and longitude coordinates. This gives you full control while still getting accurate forecasts.

Example: San Francisco is approximately 37.77 latitude, -122.42 longitude.

Find your coordinates

Type your city and we'll search for the latitude and longitude in decimal degrees.

Pro tip: After changing settings, check your actual solar production against the forecast for a few days. If the forecast is consistently too high or low, try adjusting your Performance Ratio up or down by 0.05 until it aligns with reality.

Still unsure about a setting? Check your solar installer's paperwork or your inverter's display or monitoring app.

Is the forecast significantly off? If your actual solar production consistently differs from OnSun's prediction, we'd like to hear from you. Send screenshots of your solar app alongside OnSun's forecast, along with your configuration details and location. This data helps us improve the forecasting model.
